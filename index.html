About Diana

Diana Chau is a pianist, music educator, and artist. She received her Bachelor of Arts degree in Piano Performance from the University of California, Santa Cruz and completed her Master of Music degree in Piano Pedagogy and Performance from the University of Oklahoma. In 2014, she performed a full-length solo recital, debuted a performance in Austria, and appeared as concerto soloist in UC Santa Cruz. Her former piano teachers include Dr. Jeongwon Ham, Dr. Stephen Beus, Mary Jane Cope, Ava Soifer, and Joyce Moy.
Ms. Chau held numerous positions in non-profit organizations such as the Music Teachers National Association (MTNA), California Association of Professional Music Teachers (CAPMT), and Music Teachers’ Association of California (MTAC), San Francisco Branch. Her most recent positions include chairing CAPMT’s Northern Festival and North Regional Concerto Competition, serving as President and Board of Director in MTAC-SF, artist-in-residence in her former high school Ruth Asawa School of the Arts High School, all while maintaining a private piano studio. As a leader in various programs, she is committed to improving the quality, accessibility, and awareness of arts education.

Teaching Philosophy

Early in my career, I discovered that mastering the art of music making is similar to learning sports, science, math, and any other skills. As with any highly-skilled activity, a desireable outcome will require time, persistence, thoughtful practice, mentorship, experience, and a lot of passion. 'Talented', 'gifted' and prodidy are common terms terms used in our culture to describe musicians. However, it is the students who seek challenges and growth that possess the greatest gift of all.

I strive to highlight my students' existing passions and channel those qualities into their music. My role is to guide my students in developing into the best version of themselves. No different than the busy daily lives of modern people, we celebrate successes, experience setbacks, and incrementally become better than we were yesterday. I invite you to channel your musical experience into your everyday experiences.
